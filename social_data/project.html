Introduction

The Afghanistan War diary released by WikiLeaks in 2010 are contained in a 371729121 byte CSV file. It contains 390849 rows and 34 columns. The columns contain dates, locations, reports, category information and counts of the killed and wounded. What is going to be presented covers:
  • D3 Barchart
  • D3/Leaflet Maps
  • D3 Wordclouds
  • Category Prediction
  • KNN
  • nltk
More in depth analysis can be found in the explainer iPython notebook. There should be download options for data sets (so the user can play around).

Voters watch out!

Elections are one of the most fundamental principles of any democracy. The people in Afghanistan who want to make a difference in their war troubled country try to go vote for a change. In the following visualization security incidents during the election in August 2009 were taken. The spots of these incidents are have an overlay of a KNN grid that shows most likely war incidents that happen in this area.

Making Predictions

Evaluate the best classifier to chose to predict the right category according to the described event in the summary. Useful for the US-Army to avoid the category "Other" or double meanings like "blue-white" and "blue-on-white" for instance. Usually every report from the military goes into the headquarter for a post analysis. Especially the "significant" activities are analyzed and evaluated by a special group in the command according to the WikiLeaks structure of the reports. Some machine learning algorithms could help them to optimize their work. We decided to do the analysis for all incidents in Kabul (RC-Capital) from 2004 to 2009.
